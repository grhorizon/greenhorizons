Cost Information:

This section is where you can check the average costs or tax benefits for using green energy. A lot of it varies depending on where you live and what's available to you. It goes over all of the clean energy methods discussed on this website.

Wind

The cost to use wind energy will vary depending on the area you're in. If you live near a wind farm, the company can give you the price of using it. Installing a wind turbine on property will vary depending on how large of a wind turbine you need. Small wind turbines can cost anywhere from $3,000 to $5,000. That cost will go up depending on the size you need. You can get tax rebates on using wind turbines as a power source. Check your local municipality, state and federal guidelines for current tax rebate availability or other benefits for using this energy resource.

Solar

The cost to install solar panels is also dependent on where you live, check the prices of the companies in your area. It could cost as low as 10k or upwards of 25k depending on the area. After you install the solar panel, it's low maintenance. In using them, you'll end up with excess energy frequently, which you can then sell to power grinds, or store in batteries that you place in your house. The current solar tax credit is 30% in 2024 as long as you own solar panels.

Geothermal

For a geothermal system to be installed in your house it can cost anywhere from $17k-$25k for the average house. After being installed you're able to save a lot of money on your heating and cooling bills and your electricity bills, though you have to make the initial investment into buying the system. The current credit for using a geothermal system is 30% on taxes as long as you have an eligible system.

Nuclear

For nuclear power you need to be near a nuclear power plant. Meaning this cost will depend on the area you're in. If you live in an area with a power plant check the cost to use it. The average cost to use nuclear energy is $30.92. Check your local municipality, state and federal guidelines for current tax rebate availability or other benefits for using this energy resource.
